About

Junyi Zhou was a Ph.D. candidate in Biostatistics at Richard M. Fairbanks School of Public Health and School of Medicine, Indiana University. He is co-advised by Dr. Wanzhu Tu and Dr. Ying Zhang. His research focuses on clustering algorithms especially for longitudinal data; causal inference in potential outcome framework when multiple treatments exist; Positive-and-Unlabeled data learning methods in the situation like cancer-metastasis that only positive cases are for sure, while the rest are unlabeled. He is also experienced in real world data (RWD) analysis such as interval-censored data, longitudinal data/time series data, and high-dimensional data, and supervised learning techniques, especially for tree-based algorithms such as fandom forests, BART, Gradient boosting (XGboost), reinforcement learning trees, etc.

Prior to Ph.D., Junyi received his M.S. in Financial Engineering from Fudan University, School of Management. His thesis explored the stochastic jumping behaviors in the exchange rates, which won the best thesis award. He completed his Bachelor’s Degree at Shanghai Jiao Tong University (SJTU) in School of Physics and Astronomy.

He worked as a Research Assistant in PREDICT-HD group to analysis the 12-year Huntington Disease data collected from the lab in purpose of detecting the biomarker of Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) and the potential phenotypes of disease progression. He also served as a Research Assistant in a collaborative project between Department of Neurological Sciences Mind & Brain Health Labs in UNMC and Toyota company. In the project, he tried to profile a risk-score to evaluate the driving risk among subjects with Type 1 or Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus based on the data from GPS, GIS, Automotive Sensors, and Fitbit.

He is now working at Amgen Inc as a Statistician Manager.
